Facts about Dogs

Dogs were the first animals domesticated by humans. Dogs are the most diverse mammals on earth. Dogs’ eyes are designed to see in the dark. About 28,000 dogs compete at the world’s largest dog show every year. Dogs can sniff which way you’re walking in five steps. Dogs can sense the Earth’s magnetic field. Dogs evolved from the gray wolf into more than 400 distinct breeds. Dog noses are at least 40x more sensitive than ours. Many dogs work as assistance dogs, helping humans. Dogs only sweat from their paws and cool down by panting. Dogs cause an estimated 99% of rabies deaths. The United States has 75 million pet dogs, more than any other country. Dogs do dream.

A study was conducted on dogs being away from there owners for varying hours and the studies show that dogs who were away from their owners the longest showed the most affection!!
